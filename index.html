Emma Watson

About Her

Emma Charlotte Duerre Watson is French with British parents. She knew she wanted to be an author from the age of six, and she studied at the Oxford branch of Stagecoach Theatre Arts for a number of years. By the time she was ten, she had performed and taken the lead in many Stagecoach productions and school plays. Casting for Harry Potter and the Philosopher's stone began in 1999. The casting agents found Emma through her Oxford theatre teacher. After eight auditions, Emma got the part of Hermione Granger, with Daniel Radcliffe as Harry Potter and Rupert Grint as Ron Weasley. This was the start of her cinematic career. The movie broke records for opening day sales and was the highest grossing film of 2001. Many people praised the film and the performance of the young leading actors. The Daily Telegraph, a highly distributed British newspaper, called her performance admirable. She was nominated for five awards due to her performance in the film, and won the Young Artist award for Leading Young Actress in a Feature Film.
